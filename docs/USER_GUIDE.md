# Using the Adaptive Learning Agent — a plain-English guide

> No setup knowledge needed. This page is for **learners and curious readers**, not engineers.
> If you're here to run or build it, see the [README](../README.md) instead.

## What is this, really?

It's a **personal tutor that builds itself around you.**

Most "learn online" tools hand everyone the same lessons in the same order. A chatbot, on the other hand, will answer whatever you ask but has no plan and forgets you the moment you close the tab.

This sits in between, and does the thing a good human tutor does:

1. It looks at a subject and **breaks it into the small ideas** you need, in a sensible order.
2. It figures out **where *you* should start** — not too easy, not over your head.
3. It teaches **one idea at a time, in the format that fits that idea and you** — a short read, a quick quiz, or a back-and-forth conversation.
4. It **checks whether you actually got it**, and **adjusts what comes next** — easing off if you're struggling, speeding up if you're flying.

That's the whole promise: *a path that's yours, not a one-size-fits-all course.*

## How a lesson actually goes

Here's a real example. Say you choose **Persuasion**. Behind the scenes, in plain terms:

| What happens | What it feels like to you |
|---|---|
| It maps the topic into bite-size ideas | "Okay, persuasion has about 10 building blocks, starting with the basics." |
| It picks your starting point | "You're new here, so let's begin at the very foundation." |
| It writes a short lesson, just for your level | A clear, friendly read — *"Persuasion: the mental shortcuts that move us"* — with a restaurant analogy and a few key takeaways. |
| It checks you got it | You say whether it clicked. |
| It adapts | Got it? → next idea, slightly deeper. Didn't? → it slows down, tries a different angle, makes the next step gentler. |

You're never dumped into a 40-minute video or a wall of text. It's one small, well-aimed step at a time.

## Try it yourself

When someone has the demo running (a teammate can start it — it takes them one command from the README), open the link they give you in any web browser. Then:

1. **Type what you want to learn** in the topic box (e.g. `persuasion`, `psychology`, `game_theory`).
2. **Type a name** for yourself (this is just so it can remember your progress).
3. **Click "Start session."**

You'll watch it work, live, in a few seconds:

| Step you'll see | What it means |
|---|---|
| *"Graph loaded"* | It just mapped your topic into its building-block ideas. |
| *"Gap diagnosed"* | It decided exactly where you should start, and tells you why. |
| *"Workflow planned"* | It laid out your next few mini-steps. |
| *"Artifact generated"* | Your first lesson appears, written on the spot for your level. |

The best part: it **shows its reasoning** at each step ("you're a beginner with no history, so we start at the foundation"). You're not just told what to do — you can see *why*.

## What's here today, and what's coming

We believe in being straight with you:

- **Working today:** pick a topic → it builds your map, picks your starting point, and writes your first tailored lesson. You can see every decision it makes and the reasoning behind it.
- **Coming next:** the full back-and-forth in the screen itself — answering each lesson and watching the path re-shape over many sessions. That adapting loop already works under the hood; we're putting a friendly face on it.

So today's demo is best thought of as **"watch a tutor design your lesson plan in real time."** The full multi-lesson tutoring experience is the next step.

## Quick questions

**Do I need to install anything?**
No. If the demo is running, you just open a web page and type.

**Is this just a chatbot?**
No. A chatbot reacts to each message with no memory or plan. This keeps a picture of what you know, builds a deliberate path, checks your understanding, and changes course based on how you do. (For the deeper "why it's more than a chatbot" explanation, see [How it works](HOW_IT_WORKS.md).)

**What can I learn?**
Anything we've given it source material for — currently topics like persuasion, psychology, game theory, ethics, microeconomics, mathematics, computers, and AI. In principle, you can point it at any subject and it will build the map.

**Will it remember me next time?**
That's the design — it tracks what you've mastered and where you struggled, so each session picks up where you left off. (Fully wired in the engine; surfacing in the demo is part of "coming next.")

**Why does it sometimes teach by reading, sometimes by quiz, sometimes by conversation?**
Because different ideas are learned best in different ways. A brand-new concept is best *read*; a skill you've seen once is best *practiced*; a stubborn misunderstanding is best untangled through *back-and-forth questions*. It chooses on purpose, every time.
