# Psychology

## System 1 vs. System 2

Kahneman's dual-process model: System 1 is fast, automatic, intuitive; System 2 is slow, deliberate, effortful. Most everyday cognition runs on System 1; biases creep in when System 1 answers a question System 2 should be answering.

## Fundamental attribution error

The tendency to explain others' behavior in terms of disposition ("she's selfish") while explaining one's own behavior in terms of situation ("I had a bad day"). The asymmetry is systematic and culturally robust.

## Confirmation bias

The tendency to seek, weigh, and remember evidence that confirms an existing belief while discounting evidence against it. Affects what you Google, what you click, what you remember.

## Cognitive dissonance

The mental discomfort of holding two contradictory beliefs, or acting against a belief. People resolve it by changing the belief — usually the easier-to-change one. Festinger's cult-prophecy study is the classic case.

## Hedonic adaptation

Humans return to a baseline level of happiness despite positive or negative life events. Lottery winners and accident victims, six months out, are roughly as happy as their pre-event baselines. The "set point" is real but slowly shiftable.

## Loss aversion

Losses feel roughly twice as bad as equivalent gains feel good. A core mechanic behind status-quo bias, the endowment effect, and most failed product pricing.

## Social proof

People copy the behavior of others as a heuristic for what's correct, especially under uncertainty. Restaurants leave queues out front for a reason. Closely related to conformity (Asch line studies) and herd behavior.
